Dal Baati Churma Recipe

Dal Baati Churma is a traditional and iconic dish from Rajasthan, India. It consists of three components: dal (lentils), baati (baked wheat balls), and churma (sweet crumbled mixture).

Ingredients

For the Dal:

  • ½ cup Toor dal (pigeon peas)
  • ¼ cup Chana dal (Bengal gram)
  • ¼ cup Moong dal (yellow lentils)
  • 2 tbsp Ghee (clarified butter)
  • 1 tsp Cumin seeds
  • A pinch of Hing (asafoetida)
  • 2 Green chilies, chopped
  • 1-inch piece Ginger, grated
  • 1 Onion, finely chopped
  • 2 Tomatoes, finely chopped
  • ½ tsp Turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp Red chili powder
  • 1 tsp Coriander powder
  • Salt to taste
  • ½ tsp Garam masala
  • Coriander leaves for garnish

For the Baati:

  • 2 cups Whole wheat flour
  • ¼ cup Semolina (optional)
  • ½ cup Ghee (melted)
  • 1 tsp Carom seeds (ajwain)
  • Salt to taste
  • Water as needed

For the Churma:

  • 2-3 Crumbled baatis
  • ¼ cup Powdered sugar or jaggery
  • 3-4 tbsp Ghee
  • Chopped dry fruits (almonds, cashews, raisins)

Instructions

1. Preparing the Dal

  1. Wash and soak the dals for 30 minutes.
  2. Pressure cook the dals with 2-3 cups of water, turmeric, and salt for 2-3 whistles until soft. Mash slightly.
  3. Heat ghee in a pan, add cumin seeds and hing. Let it splutter.
  4. Add green chilies, ginger, and sauté for a minute.
  5. Add onions and cook until golden. Then, add tomatoes and cook until soft.
  6. Mix in red chili powder, coriander powder, and garam masala.
  7. Pour in the cooked dal and adjust the consistency with water. Simmer for 5-7 minutes.
  8. Garnish with coriander leaves and set aside.

2. Preparing the Baati

  1. Mix wheat flour, semolina, ajwain, and salt in a bowl.
  2. Add melted ghee and mix until crumbly.
  3. Knead into a firm dough using water. Rest for 15-20 minutes.
  4. Preheat the oven to 180°C (350°F).
  5. Shape the dough into medium-sized balls and place them on a baking tray.
  6. Bake for 20-25 minutes, turning occasionally, until golden and crispy. Alternatively, cook in a tandoor or over direct flame.
  7. Dip the hot baatis in melted ghee before serving.

3. Preparing the Churma

  1. Take 2-3 baked baatis and crumble them finely.
  2. Heat ghee in a pan and add the crumbled baati. Roast on low flame until golden brown.
  3. Mix in powdered sugar or grated jaggery and dry fruits.

Serving Dal Baati Churma

Serve the dal hot in a bowl, the baatis broken into pieces and drizzled with ghee, and the churma on the side as a sweet accompaniment. Enjoy with extra ghee for a royal Rajasthani experience!
