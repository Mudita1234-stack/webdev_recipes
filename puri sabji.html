Puri Sabji Kachori Recipe

Ingredients

For Puri:

  • 1 ½ cups whole wheat flour
  • 2 tbsp semolina (rava)
  • 1 tbsp oil
  • Salt to taste
  • Water as needed
  • Oil for frying

For Sabji:

  • 2 medium potatoes, boiled and mashed
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 1 tomato, finely chopped
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

For Kachori Filling:

  • 1 cup moong dal (yellow lentils), soaked for 2 hours
  • 1 tbsp oil
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 tsp fennel seeds
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1 tsp cumin powder
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • Salt to taste

Instructions

For Puri:

  1. In a large bowl, mix whole wheat flour, semolina, and salt.
  2. Add oil and mix well to form a crumbly texture.
  3. Gradually add water and knead into a smooth dough.
  4. Cover the dough and let it rest for 15-20 minutes.
  5. Divide the dough into small balls and roll them out into small discs (puris).
  6. Heat oil in a deep frying pan and fry the puris until golden and puffed up.
  7. Remove and drain excess oil on a paper towel. Set aside.

For Sabji:

  1. Heat oil in a pan, add cumin seeds, and let them splutter.
  2. Add chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.
  3. Stir in ginger-garlic paste and cook for 1-2 minutes.
  4. Add chopped tomatoes, turmeric, red chili powder, and garam masala. Cook until the oil separates.
  5. Add boiled and mashed potatoes. Mix well and cook for 5-7 minutes, adjusting salt as needed.
  6. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and keep aside.

For Kachori Filling:

  1. Heat oil in a pan, add cumin seeds and fennel seeds. Let them splutter.
  2. Add soaked and drained moong dal and cook for 2-3 minutes.
  3. Stir in coriander powder, cumin powder, red chili powder, and salt. Cook for 5-7 minutes.
  4. Remove from heat and let it cool. Once cooled, grind into a coarse filling paste.

Assembling the Kachori:

  1. Take a small portion of the puri dough and flatten it into a small disc.
  2. Place a spoonful of the moong dal filling in the center of the disc.
  3. Seal the edges to form a ball and roll it out gently into a flat kachori.
  4. Heat oil in a pan and deep fry the kachoris until golden brown and crispy.

Serving

Serve the hot puris, sabji, and crispy kachoris together for a delicious meal. Enjoy with chutney or yogurt!

Bon Appétit! Enjoy your Puri Sabji Kachori!
